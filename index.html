--- layout: home title: Stone Journal Text Analysis Project subtitle: Project Introduction --- Introduction

The goal of my project is to explore the use of text analysis tools on scanned primary source journals that have been processed with optical character recognition (OCR) to see what kind of useful information can be obtained through these methods and how helpful this process can be as a research tool. My specific research interest in conducting these investigations is to explore issues of health and safety in the stone industry during 1888 and 1922 as they were discussed in one of its primary industry journals, Stone: An Illustrated Magazine that has been digitized and is available through HathiTrust Digital Library. While my own personal research focus has been on health and safety there is also substantial historical research on the emergence of environmental awareness and advocacy in the same time period and I used this project for some basic investigations around the language of environmental issues as well.

This project is driven by the questions of how employers within the stone industry approached workplace safety, health, and environmental issues. Is this transition visible within the written record of the stone industry's general publications? Was it a serious concern that was often discussed between multiple employers within the industry or were these discussions something that didn't get a lot of attention in the general record?

There are two intended audiences for this project. First, people interested in historical developments in industrial health, safety, and environmental issues who might be interested in the information found during the study. Second, researchers who may be considering their own text analysis projects are interested in the techniques and methods I used during my investigations.
